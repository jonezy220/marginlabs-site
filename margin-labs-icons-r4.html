01 — Additive Margin Layer · The Right Concept

Margin here is incremental profitability — a layer added on top of an existing business through payments or optimization. The icon should feel like something being added, built up, or unlocked — not a before/after gap. These concepts treat the bars as a compounding P&L, not a comparison chart.

P1 — Added Layer

Added Layer
Each bar has two parts: a dim base (existing business) and a bright copper cap (payments margin added on top). The cap grows as the model advances — the additive layer gets bigger at each stage. Reads directly as "here's your business, here's the margin payments adds to it." No failure implied. Pure addition.
P2 — Margin Cap
64px
32px
Copper
Margin Cap
A single bar, split into base (existing business, dim) and the payments margin cap above it (bright, copper). The + mark inside the cap makes the "added" concept explicit without a second bar. The measure ticks on the left show it's a quantified addition, not just visual. Most direct single-element version of the concept.
P3 — Stacked Margin
64px
32px
Copper
Stacked Margin
Horizontal layers building upward, each brighter than the last — the top layer is full-intensity copper. Reads as revenue layers accumulating, the most recent and most valuable at the top. The intensity gradient makes "more margin added = brighter" feel intuitive. Clean and abstract — works as a general "building profitability" mark beyond just payments.
P4 — Unlocked
64px
32px
Copper
Unlocked
Three bars growing steadily (the base business), then a fourth that has both a base AND a distinct bright cap — payments margin added on top. The momentum arrow above the cap signals continued upward potential. B4's energy with P1's semantic clarity. "Your business was already growing — payments unlocked another layer on top of that."
02 — Simpler Variants · What Survives at Small Scale

Sometimes the right icon is the simplest one that still carries the idea. These three strip everything back — one strong shape, one clear idea.

S1 — Before + After
64px
32px
Copper
Before + After
Two equal-height bars — same base, but the right one has a bright cap added on top. A plus sign between them is the operator: "base business + payments margin = taller bar." Arithmetic and clean. The "before" doesn't look bad — it's healthy. The "after" is just more. No failure state.
S2 — Plus Mark
64px
32px
Copper
Plus Mark
Four ascending bars, each with a small + mark etched into the bright cap — the plus is the "added margin" signal baked into every stage. The icon reads as "each level of this is an addition, not just growth." At small sizes the + marks disappear but the two-tone bars still carry the concept cleanly.
S3 — Compound
64px
32px
Copper
Compound
Ascending bars where the bright cap (payments margin) grows proportionally larger at each stage — not just the bar getting taller, but the margin layer itself expanding. A line connecting the cap tops traces the margin trajectory. Synthesis of B4's energy with the additive cap concept. The caps compound — each model unlocks more margin per unit of business.
